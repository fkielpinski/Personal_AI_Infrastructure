# Thinking Skill

## Modes

### First Principles
Decompose any concept to its irreducible foundations. Challenge every assumption. Rebuild from axioms.

### Red Team
Adversarially attack a plan, idea, or claim. Find every flaw, edge case, and failure mode. Be brutally honest.

### Council
Simulate a multi-perspective debate between N expert personas. Each persona argues their strongest case. Synthesize after debate.

### Iterative Depth
Start broad, then go one level deeper per iteration. Useful for problems where the right question isn't yet known.

### Premortem
Assume the project/plan has already failed. Work backwards to identify what caused the failure.

## Workflow

1. **Select mode** based on the nature of the problem
2. **Apply the mode** rigorously — don't soften the analysis
3. **Surface non-obvious insights** — the value is in what isn't immediately apparent
4. **Synthesize** into actionable conclusions

## Invocation Examples

- `thinking first-principles: why does X work the way it does`
- `thinking red-team: my plan to do Y`
- `thinking council: should I use A or B for Z`
- `thinking premortem: my project to build W`
